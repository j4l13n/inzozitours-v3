11 Days Full of Congo Nile Trail

Overview

The Congo Nile Trail is divided into 10 sections, each offering its own attractions and challenges. Spanning approximately 227 kilometers, the distance of the entire trail can take anywhere from 2 to 10 days to complete, depending on the mode of transportation and the pace of travel.

Itinerary

Explore the detailed itinerary of your 11-day Congo Nile Trail adventure:

  • Section 1: Rubavu – Cyimbiri Trail

    • Starting point: BRALIRWA Brewery Ltd.

    Duration: 5 hours

    Length: 16.5 km

    Difficulty: Medium

    The 16.5 km Rubavu-Cyimbiri hike starts from Rubavu, Rwanda’s largest beach town. The trail concludes at Cyimbiri Guest House, nestled lakeside amidst coffee plantations and lush greenery. Midway, participants can engage in various community-based activities, such as traditional pottery, music, and immersive coffee and tea experiences. Accommodation options range from budget guesthouses to upscale four-star lodgings.

    Accommodation: Cyimbiri Guest House (camping is also available).

  • Section 2: Cyimbiri – Kinunu

    • Starting point: Cyimbiri Guest House

    Duration: 4 hours

    Length: 13.4 km

    Difficulty: Medium

    Spanning Lake Kivu’s periphery and weaving through small villages and harbors, the 13.4 km Cyimbiri-Kinunu stretch begins with a traditional healing trail and showcases captivating landscapes. Within this phase, visitors can also partake in community-based tourism offerings like beekeeping, coffee encounters, and engaging with local women cooperatives for handcrafting experiences.

    Accommodation: Kinunu Guest House, Rushel Kivu Lodge

  • Section 3: Kinunu – Bumba

    • Starting point: Kinunu Center

    Duration: 7 hours

    Length: 24 km

    Difficulty: Challenging

    Covering 24 km of rolling hills, the Kinunu-Bumba trail provides hikers with awe-inspiring Lake Kivu vistas. This challenging but feasible phase offers opportunities for agro-tourism experiences with local communities. The route provides intriguing lake and valley views, coupled with occasional steep and demanding stretches.

    Accommodation: Bumba Base Camp, Dian Fossey Hotel

  • Section 4: Bumba – Karongi

    • Starting point: Bumba Base Camp

    Duration: 5 hours

    Length: 15.9 km

    Difficulty: Challenging

    The 15.9 km Bumba-Karongi segment marks the northern culmination of the Congo Nile Hiking Trail. A short boat voyage precedes entry into the tranquil town of Karongi. Within this town, tourists can engage with local fishermen during night fishing outings, experience unique activities like swimming cows, sorghum, and silkworm encounters, and embark on boat rides to the lake’s hilly islands.

    Accommodation: Karongi offers a range of options, from small guest houses to larger hotels.

  • Section 5: Karongi – Mugonero

    • Starting point: Karongi Hospital roundabout

    Duration: 6 hours

    Length: 19.6 km

    Difficulty: Challenging

    The 19.6 km Karongi-Mugonero stage unfolds breathtaking views of Lake Kivu and its captivating islands. Hikers may extend their trek with a challenging ascent up Mount Karongi to the Gisovu Tea plantations, providing options to reach Nyungwe National Park or continue along the Congo Nile Hiking Trail the following day. Gisovu offers accommodation facilities and a tea experience amidst the lush tea plantations.

  • Section 6: Mugonero – Karengera

    • Starting point: L’Esperance Mugonero

    Duration: 7 hours

    Length: 22.5 km

    Difficulty: Challenging

    The extensive 22.5 km Karengera-Mugonero route meanders through hills, valleys, and fields, showcasing splendid lake and Kivu Belt road views. The journey culminates at Karengera High School. While undertaking this leg, hikers embark on a boat trip across Mugonero Bay, scaling small paths to an elevated hill overlooking the lake, meandering through rice fields, and more.

    Accommodation: Karengera High School (basic accommodation available).

  • Section 7: Karengera – Kumbya Trail

    • Starting point: Karengera High School

    Duration: 4 hours

    Length: 14.7 km

    Difficulty: Moderate

    The 14.7 km Karengera-Kumbya stretch traverses rice fields, leading to the serene Kumbya retreat center nestled on a picturesque peninsula. Birdwatchers will delight in the bird-friendly native tree forest.

    Accommodation: Kumbya Retreat Centre, Kumbya Kivu Life Eco Lodge

  • Section 8: Kumbya – Ishara Trail

    • Starting point: Kumbya peninsula entrance

    Duration: 3 hours

    Length: 13 km

    Difficulty: Moderate

    Encompassing charming lakeside paths and picturesque villages, the 13 km Kumbya-Ishara route showcases rural smiles and greetings from local communities. This segment concludes with a stroll through Kamiranzovu Valley’s rice fields.

    Accommodation: Ishara Beach Hotel in Nyamasheke, Guest houses available in Nyamasheke Town

  • Section 9: Ishara – Shangi Trail

    • Starting point: Ishara Beach Hotel

    Duration: 7 hours

    Length: 24 km

    Difficulty: Moderate

    Laying near Lake Kivu and enveloped by nature, the 24 km Ishara-Shangi stretch unveils captivating vegetation, including eucalyptus trees. Narrow footpaths weave through villages and fields, granting stunning lake views. The trail concludes at Shangi Catholic Church, where visitors can explore historical landmarks like “Richard Kandt House.”

    Accommodation: St. Martin Parish

  • Section 10: Shangi – Kamembe Trail

    • Starting point: Shangi Catholic Church

    Duration: 5 hours

    Length: 18.3 km

    The final stage, spanning 18.3 km from Shangi to Kamembe Town, immerses hikers in the vibrant Rusizi District. Kamembe Town, home to Kamembe Airport, offers various accommodation options and community-based tourism activities, including night fishing, boat rides, and cultural experiences.

What to prepare for your hiking trip to Rwanda:

  • Tourist visa: Apply online at www.migration.gov.rw before your trip to Rwanda.
  • Long-sleeved shirts and long trousers: to protect you from the sun, insects, and vegetation along the trail.
  • Waterproof, sturdy hiking boots: Choose boots that provide ankle support and are suitable for rugged terrain.
  • Sturdy gloves: These will come in handy for gripping rocks or branches during the hike.
  • Rain jacket/poncho: Be prepared for unpredictable weather. A waterproof jacket or poncho will keep you dry during rain showers.
  • Hat: Protect yourself from the sun by wearing a hat that provides shade for your face and neck.
  • Gaiters: Gaiters will help keep debris, mud, and small rocks out of your boots.
  • Insect repellent: Apply insect repellent to protect yourself from mosquitoes and other insects.
  • A warm jacket: Evenings and higher elevations can get chilly, so pack a warm jacket or fleece to layer up.

5 Days Congo Nile Trail Adventure

Overview

The Congo Nile Trail is a rare adventure travel gem. Nowhere else in sub-Saharan Africa offers such beautiful, remote, yet accessible experiences for independent travelers.

The trail runs along Lake Kivu's shore, combining accessible infrastructure with off-the-beaten-track spontaneity. Enjoy exploring the countryside, tiny villages, and interactions with locals.

Itinerary

Explore the detailed itinerary of your 5-day Congo Nile Trail adventure:

  • Day 1: Rubavu - Cyimbiri

    • Morning:

    • Begin your journey from BRALIRWA Brewery Ltd. in Rubavu.
    • Embark on the 16.5 km hike, immersing yourself in the scenic landscapes and lakeside charm of Rubavu.
    • Explore community-based activities like traditional pottery, healing rituals, and music.
    • Continue through coffee estates and green plantations.

    Afternoon/Evening:

    • Arrive at Cyimbiri Guest House on the shores of Lake Kivu.
    • Choose between camping or renting an apartment for accommodation.
    • Enjoy a tranquil evening surrounded by coffee estates and green plantations.
    • Accommodation: Cyimbiri Guest House
  • Day 2: Cyimbiri - Kinunu

    • Morning:

    • Start your 13.4 km hike from Cyimbiri Guest House.
    • Experience the traditional healing trail and take in the charming views of Kinunu's landscapes and coffee plantations.

    Afternoon/Evening:

    • Explore small villages and harbors along Lake Kivu.
    • Engage in community-based tourism with beekeeping, coffee experiences, and handcrafting with local women cooperatives.
    • Check into Kinunu Guest House or Rushel Kivu Lodge for the night.
    • Accommodation: Kinunu Guest House or Rushel Kivu Lodge
  • Day 3: Kinunu - Bumba

    • Morning:

    • Embark on a challenging 24 km hike with breathtaking views of Lake Kivu.
    • Enjoy agro-tourism activities with local communities.
    • Navigate steep and challenging hikes while taking in the beauty of the Koko-River Valley.

    Afternoon/Evening:

    • Reach Bumba Base Camp or Dian Fossey Hotel Rutsiro.
    • Rest and recharge after a day of demanding trails.
    • Accommodation: Bumba Base Camp or Dian Fossey Hotel Rutsiro
  • Day 4: Bumba - Karongi

    • Morning:

    • Begin the final stage of the northern Congo-Nile Hiking Trail, covering 15.9 km from Bumba to Karongi Town.
    • Take a short boat trip to Home Saint Jean, your accommodation in Karongi.
    • Engage in community-based activities, including night fishing, swimming cows, and sorghum beer making.

    Afternoon/Evening:

    • Explore the unique landscapes of Karongi, its bays, and islands.
    • Cross Muregeya-River on the suspension bridge, passing banana forests and diverse landscapes.
    • Choose from various community-based activities and boat rides to hilly islands.
    • Accommodation: Home Saint Jean
  • Day 5: Karongi - Kigali

    • Morning:

    • Explore additional community-based activities or take a boat ride to hilly islands.
    • Prepare for departure from Karongi Town.

    Afternoon:

    • Head back to Kigali, concluding your 5 days Congo Nile Trail hiking adventure.
    • Reflect on the diverse landscapes, cultural encounters, and memorable experiences of your 5-day journey.
    • End of the Journey.

Inclusions:

  • Accommodations
  • Dinners
  • Lunches
  • Breakfasts
  • Guiding fee

8 Days Safari Tour in Rwanda

Overview

Eight days’ safaris in Rwanda gives you remarkable experience to explore the beauty of the country of a thousand hills by enjoying wildlife, culture, history, landscapes, and outdoor experiences. In this tour, you will explore Kigali City, ranked among the top 50 cleanest cities in the world. Enjoy landscapes, outdoor activities, and history along the way to different destinations from Kigali to Nyungwe, Akagera, and Volcanoes national parks. Encounter marvelous wildlife in our iconic parks and cultural diversity.

Itinerary
  • Day 1: Arrival at Kigali International Airport and Start City Tour

    • Depending on your arrival time, relax for 1 hour at Nyandungu Eco-Park before starting the tour and receiving a briefing about the safari. Tour Kigali City, including Kigali Genocide Memorial, Parliament Museum, Kimironko market, and Nyamirambo women center until lunch. Accommodation and dinner at Serena Hotel.

  • Day 2: Full day game driving in Akagera National Park and afternoon transfer to Kigali

    • After breakfast, start game driving into the park to see all big five Safari Mammals. Enjoy the park's nature, birdlife, small animals, lakes, and other wildlife. Accommodation and dinner at Serena Hotel.

  • Day 3: Visiting King’s Palace Museum, and Afternoon Visit to Canopy Walkway

    • After breakfast, drive to Nyungwe National Park via Nyanza to visit King’s Palace Museum to learn about the Kingdom of Rwanda and the culture of Inyambo cows. Drive to Gisakura for Check-in at Nyungwe top hill view.

  • Day 4: Chimpanzee Trek and afternoon Canopy Walk

    • Early morning drive at 5:00 AM for chimpanzee trekking with a packed breakfast. In the afternoon, after lunch, do the Canopy walk at Uwinka Reception. Accommodation at Nyungwe top hill view.

  • Day 5: Transfer to Kibuye and Visit to Napoleon Islands in the afternoon transfer to Musanze

    • Early morning drive to Karongi for a Napoleon Island Visit with a boat ride. Explore historical islands with wildlife, birds, bats, and butterflies. Lunch at Cleo Hotel and then drive to Musanze town. Accommodation and dinner at Mountain Gorilla Lodge.

  • Day 6: Bisoke Hiking in Volcanoes National Park

    • After breakfast at 6:30, drive to Volcanoes National Park office. After briefing, hike Bisoke mountain and enjoy the nature of the Volcanoes NP. Accommodation and dinner at Mountain Gorilla Lodge.

  • Day 7: Gorilla Trekking in Volcanoes National Park and afternoon visit to Gorilla Guardians

    • After breakfast at 6:30, drive to Volcanoes National Park office for briefing, before trekking Mountain Gorillas and enjoying the nature of Volcanoes NP. After lunch, visit Gorilla Guardian village to enjoy a traditional wedding and other cultural experiences. Accommodation and dinner at Mountain Gorilla Lodge.

  • Day 8: Golden Monkey trekking and then transfer to Kigali

    • After breakfast at 6:30, drive to Volcanoes National Park office for briefing, before trekking Golden Monkeys and enjoying the nature of Volcanoes NP. After lunch, transfer to Kigali International Airport.

1 Day Gorilla Trekking Rwanda

Overview

The 1-day gorilla trekking in Rwanda is the shortest trip perfectly designed for visitors with a tight schedule or budget. It offers an incredible opportunity to participate in an exhilarating mountain gorilla trekking experience in Rwanda Volcanoes National Park.

Itinerary

Leave Kigali early enough to reach Volcanoes National Park in Ruhengeri, Northwestern Rwanda. The drive time is approximately 2-3 hours, offering a scenic view of the picturesque countryside.

Upon arrival, meet the park official at Kinigi for a briefing and allocation to a gorilla family. The actual trek commences at exactly 8:00 am. Gorilla trekking can take between 2-6 hours, with 1 hour spent in close encounters with the gorillas.

After the trek, meet your driver guide for the transfer back to Kigali, marking the end of your 1-day gorilla trekking adventure in Rwanda.

Volcanoes National Park spans 160 km² and is home to mountain gorillas, golden monkeys, duikers, bushbucks, giant forest hogs, spotted hyenas, forest elephants, buffaloes, and more.

Tour Includes:

  • Transport in a 4x4 Land Cruiser
  • Mineral water and refreshments
  • Gorilla permit
  • Guiding services

1 Day Akagera Wildlife Safari

Overview

One day Akagera wildlife safari will reward you with a great encounter with a number of wildlife that this park harbors. The park is known for its great wildlife, birds, and plant species. It has recently been stocked with rhinos and lions, making it a hotspot for Big Five Game Park.

Itinerary

On your safari day, your guide will meet you at your hotel in Kigali at 5:30 am for a short briefing before you drive off to Akagera National Park. Located 2 hours away, close to the Tanzania border in the Eastern side of the country, Akagera offers a great en route drive with stopovers along the way.

By early 8 am, you will be at the park gate to register and kick off your game drive in search of wildlife such as buffaloes, impalas, elephants, zebras, giraffes, and more. The drive will stretch to the northern side of the park, where there is a higher concentration of wildlife. A picnic lunch will be enjoyed during the course of the day as you proceed with the game drive. In the evening, you will exit the park through the northern gate and drive back to Kigali, reaching in the evening and being dropped off at your hotel.

What's included:

  • All airport transfers
  • Transportation in a 4x4 safari vehicle
  • Park entrance fees
  • Game drive in the park
  • Picnic lunch
  • Services of English-speaking driver/guide
  • 1 liter bottle of mineral water per person per day during the safari

2 Days Nyungwe Forest Adventure Tour

Overview

Have breakfast at your hotel in Kigali, meet your guide, share a short briefing before you drive off to Nyungwe, which roughly 5 hour’s drive from Kigali, through Huye, visit King Palace museum and learn more about Rwanda Kingship and cultural history, as you drive to Huye where you will enjoy lunch, and later proceed to for canopy walk in Nyungwe forest. You will reach the park and register before heading for a canopy experience, which is roughly an hour and half walk. Later return and drive to your booked hotel for dinner and stay at Emeraude Kivu Resort.

Meal Plan: Lunch & dinner

Have very early morning breakfast and drive off to the chimpanzee trekking point in Nyungwe forest. You will be joined by the park ranger guides, who will direct you in the forest as you enjoy and informative guided nature walk, in search for the chimpanzees. Number of other wildlife and bird species are sighted along the trails. On finding the chimpanzees, an hour will be allowed with them. Later return, head for lunch, before driving back to Kigali, reaching in the afternoon, and dropped at the airport or hotel.

  • Accommodation as mentioned
  • Meals as stated
